Task Modification: Environmental Action Plan (Geography / HASS)

Standards & Focus Areas:

Std 1 — 1.5 1.6 Std 2 — 2.3 Std 3 — 3.5 Std 5 — 5.3
Claim:

I modified an existing “Environmental Change and Management” report task into a structured Environmental Action Plan scaffold to better support students with diverse learning needs, particularly around literacy, organisation, and task clarity.

Evidence:

Original BHS task sheet outlining the full Environmental Change and Management report (research plan + 800–1000 word report) and a simplified Environmental Action Plan version with clear headings, sentence stems, and step-by-step prompts.

Context:

Geography/HASS class completing an Environmental Change and Management unit. The original task required an extended written report and independent research. Several students were struggling with reading load, task complexity, and knowing how to structure their ideas. The modified Environmental Action Plan breaks the task into small, explicit sections: Introduction, Regional Overview, Stakeholders, Environmental Issues, Environmental Management, Recommendations, and Conclusion, with simple prompts and examples.

Evaluate:

This modification makes expectations more visible and reduces cognitive load by chunking the task and providing sentence starters (e.g. “Write a simple opening statement about the location you have chosen”; “List the people who care about this place and why”). It is designed to help students who need more support with reading and writing to participate meaningfully in the same assessment task. In future iterations, I will:

  • Co-create an example Environmental Action Plan with the class to model quality responses.
  • Offer two tiers of scaffold (high support and medium support) so students can gradually move towards more independence.
  • Build in a quick formative checkpoint (e.g. short draft of “Issues” + “Recommendations”) so I can check understanding and adjust teaching before the final submission.

Artifact List:

  • Original Environmental Change and Management task sheet (PDF)
  • Modified Environmental Action Plan scaffold (Doc/PDF)

Impact (to refine with your own notes/data):

  • Students have a clearer sequence of headings and prompts to follow, reducing “I don’t know where to start.”
  • The scaffold explicitly names stakeholders, issues, management, and recommendations, supporting content vocabulary.
  • You can now observe and compare how students respond using the scaffold vs. the original open-ended report format.

Next Steps (linked to Focus Areas):

  • 1.5 / 1.6: Develop additional versions of the scaffold (word banks, visuals, and exemplars) tailored to specific learner groups.
  • 2.3: Add explicit links back to curriculum language (e.g. “ecosystem services”, “sustainability”) within each section.
  • 3.5 / 5.3: Use students’ draft sections (“Issues” and “Recommendations”) as a formative check to adjust teaching and provide feedback before the final submission.
